## SWP Title
Occupancy and Work Within the SAIL Temperature Controlled Room (Room 116B)

**Prepared by:** Chris Betters
**Responsible supervisor/s:** Sergio Leon-Saval, Chris Betters
**Location:** Room 116B, Building A28, School of Physics
**Research Area:** Sydney Astrophotonic Instrumentation Labs (SAIL)
**Supersedes:** RA-SWP-A28-116B (initial issue 16/06/2016, current issue 26/04/2020)

## List the Hazards and Risk Controls as per Risk Assessment

**Associated risk assessment reference:** SAIL-RA-A28-116B-001

### Hazards
- Carbon dioxide accumulation in a sealed, recirculating air-conditioned room
- Failure of the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor, alarm or automatic fresh air intake
- Working in isolation within a sealed room, particularly after hours
- Door closed or latched while a person is inside
- Electric shock from flood water on the laboratory floor
- Slips on wet flooring

### Risk Controls
- **Main door to remain open whenever the room is occupied** — primary control
- CO<sub>2</sub> monitor triggering automatic fresh air intake before levels become unsafe
- Red mushroom panic button installed in the room to sound an alarm
- Notification of supervisor or appropriate person that work is occurring in the lab
- Socket outlets RCD-protected and mounted above floor level
- Flood emergency procedure including evacuation of affected areas
- General SAIL induction and specific 116B induction
- Evacuate immediately on CO<sub>2</sub> alarm

## List Resources Required

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

No PPE is required for occupancy of the room itself. The controls for this room are engineering and administrative, not PPE-based.

- PPE required by the SWP of any **equipment** operated inside the room applies as normal
- Enclosed footwear as per general laboratory rules
- Note: no respiratory protection is provided or appropriate for CO<sub>2</sub> — a filter cartridge does not protect against CO<sub>2</sub> or oxygen displacement. **Evacuation is the only response.**

### Equipment and Materials

- Mobile phone (carried, for emergency calls and check-ins)
- Door wedge or hold-open device
- Access credentials for Room 116B

### Safety Equipment (must be present in laboratory)

- CO<sub>2</sub> monitor with automatic fresh air intake — operational
- Red mushroom panic button — unobstructed and reachable from the work position
- Door openable from the inside
- Warning signage on the door (door-open rule, CO<sub>2</sub> risk)
- Nearest fire extinguisher and emergency exit — location known
- First aid kit: same level as laboratories, near parents room

### Ventilation/Environmental Controls

- Room 116B is a **sealed room with recirculating air-conditioning** for thermal stability. It has no continuous outside-air supply while the door is closed.
- Fresh air is introduced **automatically** when the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor detects rising levels.
- The **open door is the reliable source of fresh air.** The CO<sub>2</sub> monitor is a backstop, not a licence to close the door.

## Step by Step Instructions for Undertaking the Task

### BEFORE STARTING - CRITICAL SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

- **NEVER work in Room 116B with the door closed.**
- If the door must be closed to hold temperature, **you must leave the room.**
- **NEVER** silence, bypass or obstruct the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor, fresh air intake or panic button.
- **NEVER** stack equipment or stored items in front of the panic button.
- If the CO<sub>2</sub> system is faulty or under maintenance, **do not occupy the room for extended work.**
- Evacuate immediately if the CO<sub>2</sub> alarm sounds. Do not investigate first.
- Do not work alone in the room if you feel unwell.

### 1. PRE-ENTRY SAFETY CHECKS (MANDATORY - DO NOT SKIP)

1. **Check the floor before entering:**
   - Look for standing water or signs of water ingress
   - If water is present: **do not enter.** Go to Emergency Procedures — Flood Water.

2. **Check the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor:**
   - Confirm the monitor is powered and not displaying a fault or alarm condition
   - If in alarm: do not enter. Notify supervisor and building manager.
   - If faulty, blank, or showing a sensor error: do not occupy for extended work. Report it.

3. **Check the panic button:**
   - Confirm the red mushroom button is visible, unobstructed and reachable from where you will be working
   - If equipment blocks it, clear the access before starting work

4. **Secure the door open:**
   - Open the main door fully
   - Fit the hold-open device or wedge so it cannot swing or be pushed closed
   - Confirm the door can be opened from the inside

5. **Notify someone:**
   - Notify your supervisor or another appropriate person that you are working in the lab
   - State expected duration
   - For extended or after-hours work, agree a check-in time
   - Carry your mobile phone

6. **Note your equipment's own requirements:**
   - Any instrument operated inside 116B has its own SWP — complete its pre-operation checks as well

### 2. ENTERING FOR A BRIEF EQUIPMENT CHECK (door closed)

Brief, door-closed entry is permitted **only** for checking, starting or stopping equipment.

1. Confirm the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor is not in alarm before entering
2. Enter, perform the check, and leave
3. Do not settle in to work — if the task extends beyond a brief check, open the door and secure it, or leave
4. If you feel any headache, dizziness or drowsiness: leave immediately

### 3. EXTENDED WORK IN THE ROOM (door open)

1. **Confirm the door is secured open** before starting. This is not optional.
2. Carry out the work per the relevant equipment SWP
3. **Monitor yourself** throughout for CO<sub>2</sub> symptoms: headache, dizziness, drowsiness, rapid breathing, difficulty concentrating
4. If any symptom appears: **leave the room immediately** and move to fresh air. Report it — do not dismiss it.
5. If the CO<sub>2</sub> alarm sounds: evacuate immediately (see Emergency Procedures)
6. Do not allow anyone to close the door while you are inside
7. Meet any agreed check-in times

### 4. THERMAL STABILITY WORK REQUIRING A CLOSED DOOR

Where an experiment requires the door closed for thermal stability:

1. Complete all in-room work first, with the door open
2. **Leave the room**
3. Close the door from outside
4. Verify no one else is inside before closing — check visually, do not assume
5. Monitor and control the experiment remotely where possible
6. To return, follow the pre-entry checks from Section 1 again

### 5. COMPLETION AND LEAVING

1. Complete or safely pause work per the equipment SWP
2. Check no other person remains in the room
3. Remove the door hold-open device and close the door (the room is now unoccupied — the door is closed for thermal stability)
4. Confirm the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor is operating and not in fault
5. Confirm the panic button remains unobstructed
6. Notify your supervisor or check-in contact that you have finished, if a check-in was agreed
7. Report any fault, alarm activation or water ingress observed during the session

## Emergency Shutdown Procedures

The room has no process requiring emergency shutdown. **The emergency action for Room 116B is evacuation of the room, not shutdown of plant.**

### EVACUATE THE ROOM - Use in any of these situations:

- CO<sub>2</sub> alarm sounds
- You experience headache, dizziness, drowsiness or rapid breathing
- Another occupant appears confused, drowsy or unresponsive
- Water appears on the floor
- Smoke, fire or burning smell
- Building fire alarm
- CO<sub>2</sub> monitor fails or shows a fault while you are inside

### EVACUATION PROCEDURE:

1. **Leave the room immediately** — move to fresh air in the corridor
2. Press the red mushroom panic button on the way out **if a person is affected or trapped**
3. Close the door behind you once the room is unoccupied
4. Do NOT re-enter to retrieve items
5. Isolate power to room equipment at the distribution board **only if safe and dry to do so**
6. Notify supervisor and building manager
7. Restrict access until cleared
8. Call 000 if anyone is unconscious, unresponsive or has symptoms beyond mild

### RETURNING TO THE ROOM (only after the emergency is resolved):

- Do NOT enter until CO<sub>2</sub> levels are confirmed normal
- Supervisor or building manager must verify the system is operational
- Investigate and record the cause (door left closed, sensor malfunction, ventilation failure)
- Report via RiskWare
- Extended work must not resume until all safety systems are verified functional

## Emergency Procedures for Fires, Spills or Exposure to Hazardous Substances

### CO<sub>2</sub> ALARM:

1. **Evacuate the room immediately** — do not investigate first
2. Close the door behind you once the room is empty
3. Do NOT re-enter to retrieve items
4. Notify supervisor and building manager immediately
5. Restrict access to the room until the alarm clears
6. Do NOT enter until CO<sub>2</sub> levels are confirmed normal and the system verified operational
7. Investigate the cause
8. Report via RiskWare

### CO<sub>2</sub> EXPOSURE (headache, dizziness, confusion, rapid breathing, loss of consciousness):

1. **Remove the person from the room immediately if safe to do so**
   - Do NOT enter a room with an active CO<sub>2</sub> alarm alone
   - Secure the door fully open first
   - If you cannot reach them safely, call 000 and wait for emergency services
2. Move the person to a fresh air area
3. **If unconscious: call 000 immediately** and begin first aid if trained
4. Loosen tight clothing
5. Keep the person calm and still
6. Monitor breathing and consciousness
7. Seek medical attention for moderate to severe symptoms
8. Report to supervisor and via RiskWare

**Note:** There is no respirator or breathing apparatus available for CO<sub>2</sub> in this room. Do not attempt a rescue that requires you to remain in a hazardous atmosphere.

### FLOOD WATER / ELECTRIC SHOCK:

1. **Do NOT enter the room or step into standing water**
2. Isolate power to the room at the distribution board, **if the board is safe and dry to reach**
3. Evacuate the affected area and prevent others from entering
4. Notify Facilities and the building manager immediately
5. **If a person is in contact with electricity:**
   - Do NOT touch them
   - Isolate the supply first
   - Call 000
   - Once isolated and the person is clear, begin first aid if trained; commence CPR if trained and required
6. All persons who receive an electric shock **must seek medical attention**, even if they appear uninjured
7. Equipment exposed to water must be tagged out and inspected by a qualified electrician before reuse
8. Report via RiskWare

### PANIC BUTTON ACTIVATION:

1. The red mushroom button sounds an alarm to summon assistance
2. Responders must NOT enter alone if a CO<sub>2</sub> alarm is also active
3. Secure the door fully open before entering
4. Call 000 if the person is unconscious or unresponsive
5. Notify the supervisor

### FIRE EMERGENCY:

1. Evacuate the room
2. Activate the building fire alarm
3. Call 000
4. Do not re-enter
5. Follow the building evacuation procedure and fire warden instructions
6. Proceed to the designated assembly point
7. Only fight a small fire if safe to do so and you are trained

### EQUIPMENT MALFUNCTION:

1. Cease work in the room
2. Do NOT continue occupancy with a failed CO<sub>2</sub> monitor, fresh air intake or panic button
3. Tag the room or system as defective
4. Notify supervisor and building manager
5. Arrange qualified technician inspection
6. Do not resume extended work until safety systems are verified functional

## Clean Up and Waste Disposal Requirements

Occupancy of the room generates no waste and requires no specific clean up.

### AFTER EACH USE:

- Remove personal items and any temporary equipment
- Keep the area around the panic button clear
- Keep the door swept clear so it can be secured open
- Return the door wedge or hold-open device to its storage point
- Do not leave items on the floor that would obstruct evacuation or be affected by water ingress

### WASTE DISPOSAL:

- N/A for room occupancy
- Waste generated by equipment operated inside the room is covered by that equipment's own SWP

### PERIODIC MAINTENANCE:

- CO<sub>2</sub> sensor calibration and fresh-air-intake **function test** (not power-on check) — quarterly, Facilities
- Panic button function test, and confirmation of where the alarm reports to — quarterly, Facilities / Laboratory Manager
- Verify the door opens from the inside — quarterly
- Confirm signage is present and legible on both faces of the door — quarterly
- RCD testing and equipment test-and-tag per AS/NZS 3760 — Facilities
- Report drainage or water ingress faults immediately, do not wait for the schedule

## Competency Required

### MANDATORY TRAINING (Must be completed before authorisation):

1. **General SAIL induction**
2. **Specific Room 116B induction**, covering:
   - Why the room is sealed and what recirculating air-conditioning means for the atmosphere
   - The door-open rule and why it is the primary control, not a convenience
   - What to do when thermal stability requires a closed door (leave the room)
   - Location, function and reading of the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor
   - Location and operation of the red mushroom panic button, and where the alarm reports to
   - Recognition of CO<sub>2</sub> exposure symptoms in yourself and others
   - CO<sub>2</sub> alarm response — evacuate, do not investigate
   - Flood water and electric shock response, and the power isolation point
   - Lone working notification and check-in expectations
   - That no respiratory protection is available or appropriate for CO<sub>2</sub>
3. **Equipment-specific training** for any instrument operated within the room, per its own SWP

### COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT:

The user must demonstrate, in the room:

- Correct pre-entry checks — floor, CO<sub>2</sub> monitor, panic button, door secured open
- Locating and describing operation of the panic button from the work position
- Correct statement of the door rule, including the closed-door case
- Correct description of CO<sub>2</sub> symptoms and the required response
- Correct CO<sub>2</sub> alarm response
- Correct flood water response, including not entering
- Correct notification and check-in procedure for solo work

### ONGOING REQUIREMENTS:

- Refresher at induction renewal, or after any incident or alarm activation in 116B
- Re-brief all users if the CO<sub>2</sub> system, panic button, or door arrangement changes
- Report all alarm activations, faults and near misses via RiskWare

### RECORD KEEPING:

- Induction and competency sign-off recorded by the Laboratory Manager
- Alarm activations, faults and incidents logged and reported via RiskWare
- Function test and calibration records retained by Facilities
