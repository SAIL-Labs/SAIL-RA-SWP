**List of Legislation, Code of Practice, Australian Standards, Guidance Materials used to determine control measures**

- Work Health and Safety Act 2011
- Work Health and Safety Regulation 2017
- Safe Work Australia, *Confined Spaces Code of Practice* (2020) — referenced for the sealed/recirculating characteristics of the room; Room 116B is **not** classified as a confined space, see note below
- Safe Work Australia, *Workplace Exposure Standards for Airborne Contaminants* (2024) — carbon dioxide TWA 5,000 ppm (8-hour), STEL 30,000 ppm (15-minute)
- AS 1668.2-2012 — The use of ventilation and airconditioning in buildings, Part 2: Mechanical ventilation in buildings
- AS/NZS 3000:2018 — Electrical installations (Wiring Rules)
- AS/NZS 3760:2022 — In-service safety inspection and testing of electrical equipment
- AS 1851-2012 — Routine service of fire protection systems and equipment
- Safe Work Australia, *Managing the Work Environment and Facilities Code of Practice* (2018)
- University of Sydney WHS policies and procedures, including working alone / after-hours guidance
- SAIL General Laboratory Safety Risk Assessment (SAIL-RA-GENERAL-001)

**Note on confined space classification:** Room 116B is a normally-occupied workroom with a personnel door and is **not** a confined space under WHS Regulation 2017 Part 4.3. The Confined Spaces Code of Practice is referenced only as guidance on atmospheric monitoring and rescue planning, because the room shares the sealed, recirculating-atmosphere characteristic that drives the CO<sub>2</sub> hazard.

**Risk Assessment Methodology**

Assessing the risk is a brainstorming exercise, which is most effectively carried out in a team environment with the people required to complete the activity or process. Most activities or processes are broken down into a variety of separate tasks. For each task, consider the hazards, the potential harm or negative outcomes and the conditions required for those negative outcomes to occur.

Whenever assessing the health and safety risks associated with a task, always consider the following primary risk factors:

- The **physical activities** required to complete the task e.g. repetitive movement, high force, physical exertion, awkward posture
- The **work environment** e.g. lighting, layout, traffic flow, ventilation, access to support (isolation)
- The **nature of the hazard itself** e.g. working with chemicals, microorganisms, radiation, use of plant and equipment, sharps, working with potentially aggressive clients, patients or research participants
- The **people involved**, e.g. level of training, supervision, experience, health, age, physical capacity.

The information gathered from the **risk assessment** process must be used to develop a **Safe Work Procedure (SWP)** or **clinical protocol** for the activity.

**Ratings in this assessment** are derived from the likelihood/consequence pairs recorded in the hazard table using the SAIL risk matrix (Rare/Unlikely/Possible/Likely/Almost Certain × Insignificant/Minor/Moderate/Major/Severe → Low/Medium/High/Very High).

## List emergency controls
<small>These might include how to deal with fires, spills, emergency shutdown of equipment, exposure to hazardous materials and adverse reactions or the deteriorating condition of patients/research participants in our care.</small>

<div class="emergency-box" markdown="1">

### CRITICAL: Carbon Dioxide Accumulation in a Sealed Room

- **NEVER work in Room 116B with the door closed.** The door-open rule is the primary control, not a convenience.
- If the door must be closed to hold temperature, **personnel must leave the room**.
- Door-closed entry is limited to brief equipment checks — enter, check, leave.
- **NEVER** silence, bypass or obstruct the CO<sub>2</sub> monitor, its fresh air intake, or the panic button.
- **NEVER** block access to the panic button with equipment or stored items.
- If the CO<sub>2</sub> system is faulty or under maintenance, the room is not to be used for extended work.
- The room is safe for occupancy only while the door is open and the CO<sub>2</sub> and alarm systems are intact and functioning.

### Emergency Shutdown:

The room itself has no process requiring emergency shutdown; the emergency action is **evacuation of the room**, not shutdown of plant.

- Leave the room immediately and move to fresh air in the corridor.
- Press the red mushroom panic button on the way out if a person is affected or trapped.
- Isolate power to room equipment at the distribution board only if it is safe and dry to do so.
- Do not re-enter until the atmosphere is confirmed normal by the supervisor or building manager.
- Equipment within the room that has its own emergency shutdown requirement is covered by that equipment's own SWP.

### CO<sub>2</sub> Alarm Activation:

- **Evacuate the room immediately**
- Close the door behind you (the room is now unoccupied — this contains the atmosphere and lets the fresh air intake recover it)
- Do NOT re-enter to retrieve items
- Notify supervisor and building manager immediately
- Restrict access to the room until the alarm clears
- Do NOT enter until CO<sub>2</sub> levels are confirmed normal and the system is verified operational
- Investigate the cause (door left closed, sensor malfunction, ventilation failure)
- Report the incident via RiskWare

### CO<sub>2</sub> Exposure (headache, dizziness, confusion, rapid breathing, loss of consciousness):

- Remove the person from the room immediately **if safe to do so** — do not enter a room where an alarm is active without a second person present and the door secured open
- Move the person to a fresh air area
- If the person is unconscious: call 000 immediately and begin first aid if trained
- Loosen tight clothing
- Keep the person calm and still
- Monitor breathing and consciousness
- Seek medical attention for moderate to severe symptoms
- Report the incident to the supervisor and via RiskWare

### Flood Water and Electric Shock:

- Do **NOT** enter the room or step into standing water
- Isolate power to the room at the distribution board before entry, if the board is safe and dry to reach
- Evacuate the affected area and prevent others from entering
- Notify Facilities and the building manager immediately
- **If a person is in contact with electricity:** do not touch them — isolate the supply first, then call 000
- Once the supply is isolated and the person is clear, begin first aid if trained; commence CPR if trained and required
- All persons who receive an electric shock must seek medical attention, even if they appear uninjured
- Equipment exposed to water must be tagged out and inspected by a qualified electrician before reuse
- Report via RiskWare

### Panic Button Activation:

- The red mushroom panic button sounds an alarm to summon assistance
- Responders must **not** enter the room alone if a CO<sub>2</sub> alarm is also active
- Secure the door fully open before entering
- Call 000 if the person is unconscious or unresponsive
- Notify the supervisor

### Fire Emergency:

- Evacuate the room and activate the building fire alarm
- Call 000
- Do not re-enter
- Follow the building evacuation procedure and fire warden instructions
- Proceed to the designated assembly point

### Equipment Malfunction or Safety System Failure:

- Cease work in the room
- Do NOT continue occupancy with a failed CO<sub>2</sub> monitor, fresh air intake or panic button
- Tag the room or system as defective and notify the supervisor and building manager
- Arrange qualified technician inspection
- Extended work must NOT resume until all safety systems are verified functional
